Program Objective

The objective of taking our 200 hours Foundational Yoga Teacher Training is multi-dimensional. It equips learners with the knowledge, skills, and confidence to both deepen their own practice and share yoga safely and authentically with others.

9 Comprehensive Modules
200 Training Hours
3 Learning Formats

Training Modules

Nine comprehensive modules satisfying Yoga Alliance USA standards

1

Yoga Philosophy

25 Hours

Explore the philosophical foundations of yoga, including ancient texts and modern applications.

  • Eight Limbs of Yoga (Ashtanga)
  • Bhagavad Gita & Yoga Sutras
  • Ethics for Yoga Teachers
  • History & Evolution of Yoga
  • Sanskrit Terminology
2

Yoga Practices (Asana)

75 Hours

Master fundamental and advanced yoga postures with proper alignment and modifications.

  • Standing & Seated Postures
  • Backbends & Forward Folds
  • Inversions & Arm Balances
  • Sun Salutations & Sequences
  • Props & Modifications
3

Pranayama & Meditation

20 Hours

Learn breathing techniques and meditation practices for enhanced well-being.

  • Classical Pranayama Techniques
  • Breath Awareness & Control
  • Meditation Methods
  • Energy Management
  • Stress Reduction Practices
4

Anatomy & Physiology

20 Hours

Understand the body-mind connection and safe yoga practice principles.

  • Musculoskeletal System
  • Respiratory & Nervous Systems
  • Injury Prevention
  • Body Alignment Principles
  • Therapeutic Applications
5

Teaching Methodology

25 Hours

Develop effective teaching skills and classroom management techniques.

  • Class Structure & Sequencing
  • Cueing & Instruction Methods
  • Student-Teacher Relationship
  • Creating Safe Spaces
  • Handling Challenges
6

Practicum

20 Hours

Hands-on teaching experience with feedback and mentorship.

  • Practice Teaching Sessions
  • Peer Feedback & Assessment
  • Video Analysis
  • Personal Teaching Style
  • Confidence Building
7

Yoga Lifestyle & Ethics

10 Hours

Integrate yogic principles into daily life and professional practice.

  • Ethical Guidelines for Teachers
  • Professional Boundaries
  • Self-Care Practices
  • Continuing Education
  • Business Aspects of Teaching
8

Sanskrit & Chanting

10 Hours

Learn essential Sanskrit and traditional chanting practices.

  • Sanskrit Fundamentals
  • Pose Names & Meanings
  • Traditional Mantras
  • Chanting Techniques
  • Cultural Context
9

Self-Practice & Reflection

5 Hours

Develop a personal practice and reflective learning approach.

  • Creating Personal Practice Routines
  • Journaling & Self-Reflection
  • Goal Setting & Progress Tracking
  • Mindfulness Integration
  • Lifelong Learning Approach

"Teaching Yoga, Touching Lives"

As part of our Yoga Teacher Training, students engage in community service through free yoga sessions and outreach activities. This hands-on experience allows you to practice your teaching skills while making a positive impact in your community.

On completion, you will be awarded a Community Service Certificate recognizing your contribution to spreading wellness and the yogic lifestyle.
